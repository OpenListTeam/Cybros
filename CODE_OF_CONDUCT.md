# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This project is a professional, respectful, and inclusive open source
community. Everyone who participates is expected to help keep the project
welcoming, productive, and safe for contributors and users with different
backgrounds, identities, experience levels, and perspectives.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat others with empathy, patience, and respect.
- Welcome different viewpoints, priorities, and experience levels.
- Give useful feedback and accept constructive feedback in good faith.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologize when appropriate, and adjust
  behavior.
- Prioritize the long-term health of the project and its community.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention, or inappropriate
  personal comments.
- Insults, slurs, demeaning language, personal attacks, or inflammatory
  political attacks.
- Publishing private information, including addresses, contact details, or
  private messages, without clear permission.
- Abuse of maintainer privileges, project infrastructure, or moderation
  processes.
- Any conduct that maintainers reasonably judge to be inappropriate in a
  professional open source setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including repositories, issues,
pull requests, discussions, chat, events, and any other spaces where people
interact as part of this project. It also applies when someone officially
represents the project in public.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the project maintainers through the private contact
channel listed by the repository.

If no dedicated reporting channel is documented, contact the maintainers
privately through the repository or organization contact information. Do not
post sensitive conduct reports publicly.

Reports should include relevant links, screenshots, dates, context, and the
names or handles involved when that information is safe to share. Maintainers
will handle reports promptly, fairly, and with attention to reporter privacy
and safety.

## Enforcement Principles

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.
They may edit, hide, reject, or remove comments, commits, issues, pull
requests, discussions, or other contributions that violate it.

Enforcement decisions should be fair, proportionate, and documented. When
appropriate, maintainers should explain the rule violated, the expected change
in behavior, and the consequences of continued violations.

## Enforcement Ladder

Maintainers may choose any response that fits the impact and urgency of the
incident. Serious cases can skip directly to stronger actions.

1. No action: The report does not show a violation, or there is not enough
   information to act.
2. Correction: Private clarification or a request to change specific behavior.
3. Warning: A written warning that explains the violation and expected future
   conduct.
4. Cooling-off period: A temporary restriction from specific discussions,
   spaces, or people involved in the report.
5. Temporary ban: A time-limited ban from project interaction, including
   issues, pull requests, discussions, events, or project-controlled channels.
6. Permanent ban: A permanent ban from project interaction when behavior is
   severe, repeated, threatening, or unsafe for the community.
